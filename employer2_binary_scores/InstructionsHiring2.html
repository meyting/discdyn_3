Your Task:

As in part I, on the following pages, you will again be asked to make a series of {{C.number_of_pairs}} selection decisions. In each decision you will be presented with two `workers' and asked to select one of them.

The workers are participants of another study in which they have performed a task, that determines your bonus payment. You will be provided with a piece of information about each workers' performance in this task.

The task that the workers have performed measures their logical reasoning ability. In particular, they had {{C.time_limit_sequences}} minutes to solve as many number sequences as possible. In order to solve a sequence, they needed to find the missing number of the respective sequence, by detecting the pattern according to which the sequence is constructed (see example below).

Example of the Worker's Task:



In this example, the rule according to which the sequence of numbers is constructed is to subtract 1 from the previous number in the sequence. Therefore, the correct answer would be "5". The workers could always skip a sequence by clicking on "Next".

The total number of solved sequences determined a workers 'score'. The workers were incentivized by being paid according to their score, i.e. the higher a worker's score, the higher the worker's payment.

Your Bonus Payment:

One of the {{C.NUM_ROUNDS}} selection decisions will be randomly drawn as the decision-that-counts. If you have chosen the worker with the higher score in that decision, you get {{C.bonushiring}}, otherwise you get no bonus payment.

For each selection decision, we provide you with information about the workers' gender, age, race and a hint about the workers' scores.

Please click 'Next' if you have read and understood the explanations. On the next page, you will be presented with the first pair of workers.
